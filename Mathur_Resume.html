My Engineering Academy Experience:

During my time in the engineering program, I have met a number of industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers. By meeting these highly experienced and influential people I have learned a lot about different career paths, fields of study, and disciplines of engineering. So far, I have met a nuclear engineer, a machine learning engineer, geo-tech engineers, a civil engineer, aerospace engineers, and many biotech engineers.


So far, my most influential and memorable field trip was my trip to Roche where I explored biotech engineering, engineering applications with medicine, how buisnesses are started, and even how to negotiate a deal. This field trip had a unique way of showing us around and introducing us to new concepts. By splitting up into groups we got to meet different people from different positions in the company. I got to meet people who worked on the financial and management side, people who worked directly with machinery, and even people who actually work in labs with viruses and medicine. While our group was with the management and financial workers at Roche they made us do a negotiation activity where I got to be one of the two lead negotiatiors. I immediately took lead and solved the negotiation within a record time. This really spiked my interests in finance and communications and introduced me to a cool new career path. Also, as we toured the building and labs I learned a lot about the very complicated machines and technology in use which had helped solve tough problems and make jobs more efficient. Overall, this field trip broadened my view of engineering jobs and helped me see how my skills might influence the jobs I get in the future.


The guest speaker who I learned the most from and was most influenced by was Maynard Holliday, who worked for Barack Obama during his presidency and combined nuclear, electrical, and mechanical engineering to create a robot which worked in Cherynobl after the meltdown. Before meeting Mr.Holliday we learned about nuclear chemistry in our Honors Chemistry class and I was incredibly interested in it. That is why I thoroughly enjoyed Mr.Holliday's lecture and story. He expalained the terrible meltdown that occured in Cherynobl in great detail and I understood everything he was saying. I have always had my interests spread in multiple different fields so seeing how Mr.Holliday worked with so many different engineering disciplines and combined them in such a unique way inspired me. His job was exactly the type I would like to have when I graduate college, incredibly hands-on and never boring.
