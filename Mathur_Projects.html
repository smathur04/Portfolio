Python Web App: What’s For Homework?

May 6, 2019 - May 20, 2019
AP Computer Science Principles

What’s For Homework is a data gathering tool that automatically uses an algorithm to scrape data from teacher websites and google classroom to centralize a location for students to find out what homework they have for the day. Students log into a login page connected with Gmail and will be prompted to complete a one-time setup/account registration which would involve them listing their classes and respectively inputting their teacher website and/or their link to Google Classroom. Once done on initiation, the program will forever associate that student’s login with their setup schedule. Now, every time the student signs in What’s For Homework scrapes the data from the websites and lists them in an agenda for the day, creating a pre-organized agenda for the student which saves them time and effort. Along with its time-saving benefits, it will also prevent students from unnecessarily forgetting to do or turn in work. I used my extensive knowledge of Python, HTML, CSS, and libraries like Flask and Beautiful Soup to deliver a stunning website prototype that excellently displayed the possibilities and future of this product. I was the group leader and worked mainly on the code and presentation, spending countless hours trying to figure out the most feasible way to showcase the ideas of our group. I studied the arts of web scraping and web site building using Python’s libraries along with HTML and worked with my partner well to create an excellent final product.
